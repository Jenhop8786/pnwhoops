Pacific Northwest Hoops is a project long in the making. I’ve been dreaming about covering basketball in the area since I was in high school and I knew that my love for the game was only eclipsed by my love of hoops in the Northwest.

Fast forward a few years (maybe more than I would like to remember), some championships, some heartbreaks, lots of goals accomplished, and I found myself coaching and recruiting players instead of being one. My recruiting region was whole West Coast, but I found myself pointed back to my roots (perhaps to the annoyance of my bosses); I couldn’t talk enough about our guys. Right then, a seed was planted, one that wouldn’t bloom for more than another half decade.

A few more years, a few more changes and I found myself coaching my daughter’s team. I never planned on it. She didn’t even start playing until the fifth grade. Yet, when the coach of her Catholic league team asked for help, I found myself enjoying it far more than I had imagined. It was a far cry from coaching men at the Division I level, yet, I found myself just as invested. A couple of years (tons of Storm and Pac-12 women’s games later) and I find myself a fan of the women’s game as well.

Now, here I am, with a vision of chronicling the story of basketball in the Pacific Northwest. It will start with Western Washington and spread: Portland, Spokane, Boise, Vancouver B.C. you’re all on my list. But so are Blaine and Bremerton, Bend and Beaverton, Pocatello and Post Falls, Prince George and Prince Rupert. So be warned, if you bounce a ball in this region, you may find yourself in these pages. If you love the game and the Pacific Northwest, you may just find yourself here too.

-Jason
